Occupancy grid mapping using Factor graphs

Inverse sensor models

  • \( p(\mapsym_i|\meas_f, \pose_f) \) is the inverse sensor model


Drawbacks

  • Cell Independence assumption is too crude
  • Inverse sensor models is often inaccurate

Forward sensor models

  • \( p(\meas_f | \map, \pose_f) \) is the forward sensor model

Advantages

  • No independent cell assumption.
  • Forward sensor models are straightforward to compute

Occupancy grid to factor graph

  • \(V\) = Map cells are variable nodes
  • \(F\) = Laser measurements are factor nodes
  • \( E = \{(i, f) : i \in V, f \in F, \text{laser $f$ passes through cell $i$}\}\)
  • Factor graph \(G = (V, F, E) \)
  • Represents probability factorization \( P(X_V) = \prod_{f \in F}\Psi(X_{\text{Nbrs}(f)}) \)

Modern inference methods

  • Metropolis Hastings for mapping (Merali2013icra)
  • Belief propagation \[ \begin{align} \bpmsg{f}{i}{l_i}{r+1} &= \sum_{\map_f \in \Omega_f: x_i = l_i}P_f(\map_f)\prod_{j \in n(f) \setminus i}\bpmsg{j}{f}{x_j}{r} \label{eq:factor2node} \\ \bpmsg{i}{f}{l_i}{r+1} &= \prod_{h \in n(i) \setminus f}\bpmsg{h}{i}{l_i}{r} \label{eq:node2factor} \end{align} \]
  • Dual decomposition \[\begin{align} \map^f &\leftarrow \arg \min\limits_{\map^f} \left( \theta_f(\map^f) + \sum\limits_{i \in n(f)}\msg{i}{f}{x^f_i} \right) \\ \msg{i}{f}{x^f_i} &\leftarrow \msg{i}{f}{x^f_i} + \frac{\alpha}{r} & \text{for disagreening nodes \(i\)} \end{align}\]

Mutual localization

Find \(R,t\) such that \begin{align} q_i &= Rp_i + t \\ \text{where }&\\ q_i \in&\, \mathbb{R}^3, p_i \in \mathbb{R}^3, \\ R \in& \text{ SO(3), } t \in \mathbb{R}^3 \end{align}

Single observation
External landmarks
Egomotion
  • No mutual observation.
  • Example: ARToolKit (Kato et al. 1999)
  • Controlled environment.
  • Corner features are error prone.
  • Example: Bundler (Snavely et al. 2006)
  • Needs accurate egomotion estimates.

Problem formulation

\begin{align} \|s_1\uvect{p}_1-s_2\uvect{p}_2\| &=\| \vect{q_1}- \vect{q_2}\| \\ \end{align}
\begin{align} \|s_2\uvect{p}_2- \vect{p_3}\| &=\| \vect{q_2}-s_3\uvect{q}_3\| \\ \| \vect{p_3}-s_1\uvect{p}_1\| &=\|s_3\uvect{q}_3- \vect{q_1}\| \end{align}

Solving for \(s_1\) \(s_2\) and \(s_3\)

\begin{align} s_1^2 + s_2^2 - 2s_1s_2\uvect{p}_1^\top\uvect{p}_2 - \|\vect{q}_1 - \vect{q}_2\|^2 &= 0 \\ % s_2^2 - s_3^2 - 2s_2\uvect{p}_2^\top\vect{p}_3 + 2s_3\vect{q}_2^\top\uvect{q}_3 + \|\vect{p}_3\|^2 - \|\vect{q}_2\|^2 &= 0 \\ % s_1^2 - s_3^2 - 2s_1\uvect{p}_1^\top\vect{p}_3 + 2s_3\vect{q}_1^\top\uvect{q}_3 + \|\vect{p}_3\|^2 - \|\vect{q}_1\|^2 &= 0 \label{eq31} \end{align}

Eliminating \(s_2\) and \(s_3\)

\begin{align} r_2(s_1) = \begin{vmatrix} c_4 & c_3 & c_2 & c_1 & c_0 & 0 \\ 0 & c_4 & c_3 & c_2 & c_1 & c_0\\ 1 & d_1 & d_0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & d_1 & d_0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & d_1 & d_0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & d_1 & d_0 \end{vmatrix} = 0. \label{eq:finalsylvester} \end{align}

This is a 8-degree polynomial with 8-roots. Which one is the right solution?

Filtering roots

\begin{align} \| \vect{p_3}-s_1\uvect{p}_1\| &=\|s_3\uvect{q}_3- \vect{q_1}\| \end{align}

\begin{align} %(s_1 + s_2)^2 - 2s_1s_2(1 + \uvect{p}_1^\top\uvect{p}_2) - \|\vect{q}_1 - %\vect{q}_2\|^2 &= 0 % \\ %(s_2 - \uvect{p}_2^\top\vect{p}_3)^2 %- (s_3 - \vect{q}_2^\top\uvect{q}_3)^2 %+ (\vect{p}_3 - \uvect{p}_2)^\top\vect{p}_3 %- \vect{q}_2^\top(\vect{q}_2 - \uvect{q}_3) &= 0\\ (s_1 - \uvect{p}_1^\top\vect{p}_3)^2 - (s_3 - \vect{q}_1^\top\uvect{q}_3)^2 + (\vect{p}_3 - \uvect{p}_1)^\top\vect{p}_3 - \vect{q}_1^\top(\vect{q}_1 - \uvect{q}_3) &= 0 \end{align}

Goal conditioned reinforcement Learning

  • Goal is "specified"
  • No exploration needed
  • State is fully specified (no partial observation)

Recall Floyd-Warshall algorithm

\[ Q(s_t, g_w) + Q(s_w, g^*) \le Q(s_t, g^*) \]

Lessons from Deep Q Networks

Deep Q Networks

  • \begin{align} r_t + \Q(s_{t+1}) = \Q(s_t) \end{align}
  • Naive Loss:
    \( \Loss = \left( r_t + \Q(s_{t+1}) - \Q(s_t) \right)^2 \)
  • Leads to unstable updates

Deep Floyd-Warshall RL

  • \begin{align} \Q(s_t, g_w) + \Q(s_w, g^*) \le \Q(s_t, g^*) \end{align}
  • Naive Loss:
    \( \Loss = (\Q(s_t, g_w) + \Q(s_w, g^*) - \Q(s_t, g^*))_+^2 \)
  • Leads to unstable updates

Strategy:

  • Maintain two networks: Target network \(\Qtgt\) and Main network \(\Q_m\)
  • Target network is such that: \( \Theta_{\text{tgt}} \leftarrow \alpha \Theta_{\text{tgt}} + \alpha \Theta_{m} \) where \(\alpha \approx 0.9\)
  • No gradient backpropagation through target network

Solution:

\[ \Loss_{\text{bellman}} = \left( r_t + \Qtgt(s_{t+1}) - \Q_m(s_t) \right)^2 \]
\[ \LossLo = \left( \Qtgt(s_t, g_w) + \Qtgt(s_w, g^*) - \Q_m(s_t, g^*) \right)^2_+ \]
\[ \LossUp = \left( \Q_m(s_t, g_w) + \Qtgt(s_w, g^*) - \Qtgt(s_t, g^*) \right)^2_+ \]

Floyd-Warshall Reinforcement Learning

\begin{align} \Q(s_t, g_w) + \Q(s_w, g^*) \le \Q(s_t, g^*) \end{align}
\begin{align} \LossLo &= \left( \Qtgt(s_t, g_w) + \Qtgt(s_w, g^*) - \Q_m(s_t, g^*) \right)_+^2 \end{align}
\begin{align} \LossUp &= \left( \Q_m(s_t, g_w) + \Qtgt(s_w, g^*) - \Qtgt(s_t, g^*) \right)_+^2 \end{align}

Loss term ablation

  • \begin{align} \Loss_{\text{bellman}} = \left( \Rew(\state_t) + \max_{\act}\discount \Qtgt(\state_{t+1}) - \Q_m(\state_t) \right)^2 \end{align}
  • \begin{align} \LossStep &= \left( \Q_m(\state_t, \goal_{t+1}) - \Rew(\state_t) \right)^2 \end{align}
  • \begin{align} \LossLo &= \left( \Qtgt(s_t, g_w) + \Qtgt(s_w, g^*) - \Q_m(s_t, g^*) \right)_+^2 \end{align}
  • \begin{align} \LossUp &= \left( \Q_m(s_t, g_w) + \Qtgt(s_w, g^*) - \Qtgt(s_t, g^*) \right)_+^2 \end{align}
